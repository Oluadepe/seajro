Severe Storms To Blast Tennessee Valley To Southeast

A potent low pressure system will move from the Mid-Mississippi Valley into the northern Mid-Atlantic, dragging an associated cold front through the Tennessee Valley, Deep South and Southeast. This cold front separates very warm, humid air from the Gulf of Mexico streaming northward ahead of the front with colder, drier air filtering in behind the front. The collision of air masses will be where thunderstorms fire up through this afternoon and evening.

The severe weather danger zones as of now are over the Tennessee Valley and Deep South, including parts of Kentucky, Tennessee, Ohio and West Virginia as well as Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, the Carolinas and the Florida Panhandle, where two lines of strong to severe thunderstorms are moving through.
